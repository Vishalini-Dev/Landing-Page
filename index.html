A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Scientific Career

Kalam played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, particularly in the development of ballistic missiles and launch vehicle technology.He was instrumental in the success of the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). Kalam was also a key figure in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power.




Presidency

Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India in 2002 with widespread support from all political parties. He served as a non-partisan figure and was known as the "People's President" due to his accessibility and humble demeanor.During his presidency, Kalam focused on inspiring the youth of India. He traveled extensively across the country, meeting students and delivering lectures that emphasized the importance of education, innovation, and national development.

"Excellence is a continuous process and not an accident."- A. P. J. Abdul Kalam
